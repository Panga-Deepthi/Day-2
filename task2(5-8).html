Sarojini Naidu

Sarojini Naidu, known as the "Nightingale of India," was a prominent freedom fighter, poet, and politician. Born in 1879, she was a key figure in the Indian independence movement and an associate of Mahatma Gandhi. Naidu played a significant role in the Civil Disobedience Movement and was the first woman to become the President of the Indian National Congress. She also served as the Governor of Uttar Pradesh, making her the first woman to hold a gubernatorial position in independent India. Her poetry, celebrated for its lyrical quality, reflects her deep love for India and its culture.
